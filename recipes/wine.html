Wine Pairing

Dinner with wine is fine

Although not a recipe, pairing wine is similar! Just like an ingredient, wine will change the flavor of the entire meal all grouped together. There's no bad choice in wine when it comes to a simple red or white. It depends on your palette for dryness and depth.

To red or to white? That is the question...

Red wine characteristics

  • Color: A dark ruby red wine can add a touch of elegance to even the most simplest of meals.
  • Tannin: This is the what gives the dryness feel and taste effect. Similar to black tea or a bit of bitterness.
  • Flavor and Aroma: The range is brilliant including fruit, floral, spice, and earth.
  • Acid: Acidity gives the tart and sour, which balances with the sweet and bitter.

White wine characteristics

  • Color: The golden hues bring a crisp or cozy feel to your dinner experience.
  • Tannin: Similar to red wines, the dryness through tannins can vary widely.
  • Flavor and Arom: Again the range is enormous though with a tendency for lighter flavors and undertones as compared to the reds.
  • Acid: Whites will tend to have more tart and sour, but keep in mind they can also be incredibly sweet.

Choosing the right wine

  1. The wine should be more acidic than the food.
  2. The wine should be sweeter than the food
  3. The wine should have the same flavor intensity as the food
  4. Red wines pair best with bold meals.
  5. White wines pair best with light meals.
  6. Bitter wines are balanced with fatty meals.
  7. White wines tend to create contrasting pairings.
  8. Red wines tend to create congruent pairings
